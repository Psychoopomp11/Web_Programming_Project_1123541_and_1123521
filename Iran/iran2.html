5. Persian Fallow Deer

Dama dama mesopotamica

Persian Fallow Deer is a beautiful and rare species of deer native to the Middle East. They are known for their large, broad antlers (in males) and their spotted coats, which provide excellent camouflage in forested and shrubland habitats. These deer are herbivores, feeding on grasses, leaves, and shrubs.

Conservation Status: Once thought to be extinct, the Persian Fallow Deer was rediscovered and has been the focus of conservation programs to reintroduce them into protected areas. They remain endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.

Fun Fact: Persian Fallow Deer are incredible jumpers and can leap over high obstacles to escape predators or explore new areas!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Persian Fallow Deer
  • Scientific Name: Dama dama mesopotamica
  • Animal Type: Mammal
  • Size: 100-120 cm (3.3-3.9 ft) at shoulder height
  • Weight: 40-100 kg (88-220 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Endangered

6.Hawksbill Turtle

Eretmochelys imbricata

Hawksbill Turtle is a stunning sea turtle known for its intricately patterned shell and sharp, hawk-like beak. These turtles are essential to marine ecosystems, as they help maintain coral reef health by consuming sponges that compete with corals.

Conservation Status: Hawksbill Turtles are critically endangered due to illegal poaching for their beautiful shells, habitat destruction, and climate change affecting nesting sites.

Fun Fact: Hawksbill Turtles can travel thousands of kilometers during their lifetime, often returning to the same beach where they were born to lay eggs!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Hawksbill Turtle
  • Scientific Name: Eretmochelys imbricata
  • Animal Type: Reptile
  • Size: 60-90 cm (2-3 ft)
  • Weight: 45-70 kg (100-154 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 30-50 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered

7. Green Turtle

Chelonia mydas

Green Turtle is a gentle giant of the ocean, named for the greenish color of its body fat due to its herbivorous diet of seagrass and algae. These turtles play a critical role in maintaining healthy seagrass beds, which are essential for many marine species.

Conservation Status: Green Turtles are found in tropical and subtropical seas worldwide, migrating long distances between feeding grounds and nesting beaches. They are endangered due to threats like habitat destruction, bycatch in fishing gear, and pollution.

Fun Fact: Green Turtles can hold their breath for several hours while resting underwater, making them true masters of relaxation!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Green Turtle
  • Scientific Name: Chelonia mydas
  • Animal Type: Reptile
  • Size: 80-120 cm (2.6-4 ft)
  • Weight: 110-180 kg (242-397 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 50-70 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Endangered
