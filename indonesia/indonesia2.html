5. Sumatran Tiger

Panthera tigris sumatrae

The Sumatran Tiger is a magnificent big cat native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra. Known for its powerful build and distinctive orange coat with black stripes, the Sumatran Tiger is one of the most endangered tiger subspecies. These tigers typically weigh between 100 to 140 kilograms (220 to 310 pounds) and can grow up to 2.8 meters (9.2 feet) in length, including the tail.

Sumatran Tigers are solitary hunters and typically prey on medium-sized mammals like deer and wild boar. However, they are also capable swimmers and have been known to hunt in the waters of rivers and lakes. These tigers are highly territorial and communicate through scent marking and vocalizations.

The Sumatran Tiger is critically endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict. Conservation efforts focus on habitat restoration and anti-poaching measures to protect the remaining tiger population.

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s):Sumatran Tiger
  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris sumatrae
  • Animal Type:: Mammal
  • Size: 2.4 to 2.8 meters (7.8 to 9.2 feet) in length (including tail)
  • Weight: 100 to 140 kilograms (220 to 310 pounds)
  • Lifespan: 10 to 15 years in the wild

6. Mentawai Macaque

Macaca pagensis.

The Mentawai Macaque, or Beruk Mentawai, is a primate found only on the Mentawai Islands off the west coast of Sumatra, Indonesia. This macaque has a unique appearance, with long, dark fur and a white ring of fur around its face, giving it a distinctive look. They are medium-sized monkeys, measuring about 45 centimeters (17.7 inches) in body length, with a tail length of around 40 centimeters (15.7 inches). They are known for their curious and social nature.

These monkeys are omnivores, feeding on fruits, leaves, and insects, as well as small animals. They live in groups and are highly active during the day, spending most of their time in the trees where they forage for food. The Mentawai Macaque is an excellent climber and uses its agility to escape predators. However, the species is facing severe threats due to deforestation and hunting, making it critically endangered.

Conservation efforts are being made to protect their habitats and limit human encroachment on the Mentawai Islands, though the species remains at risk of extinction.

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s):Mentawai Macaque
  • Scientific Name: Macaca pagensis
  • Animal Type:: Mammal
  • Size: 45 centimeters (17.7 inches) in body length
  • Weight: Around 7 kilograms (15.4 pounds)
  • Lifespan: 20 years in the wild

7. Wallace’s Standardwing

Semioptera wallacii

The Wallace’s Standardwing, or Burung Bidadari Halmahera, is a striking and rare bird endemic to the forests of Halmahera, an island in Indonesia. This bird is famous for the dramatic, long feathers that extend from its wings, giving it an almost angelic appearance during its elaborate mating displays. Males are known to showcase these feathers to attract females, performing impressive aerial displays to show off their plumage.

Wallace’s Standardwing is a medium-sized bird, measuring up to 30 centimeters (11.8 inches) in length, with vibrant colors including green, yellow, and black. It feeds primarily on fruit and insects found in the dense rainforests it inhabits. Despite its mesmerizing beauty, the species is critically endangered, facing threats from habitat destruction, particularly from logging and land conversion for agriculture.

Conservation efforts focus on protecting the remaining habitats on Halmahera and promoting sustainable practices to safeguard the future of this extraordinary bird.

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s):Wallace’s Standardwing
  • Scientific Name: Semioptera wallacii
  • Animal Type:: Bird
  • Size: 30 centimeters (11.8 inches) in length
  • Weight: 70 grams (2.5 ounces)
  • Lifespan: Unknown (likely 5 to 10 years)
