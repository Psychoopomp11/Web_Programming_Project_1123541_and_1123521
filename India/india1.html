1. Yellow-breasted Bunting

Emberiza aureola

Yellow-breasted Bunting is a small songbird known for its cheerful yellow belly and melodious calls. Males have bright yellow underparts with rich chestnut-brown feathers on their backs, while females are paler with a streaky appearance. These birds are found in grasslands, marshes, and open countryside, where they feed on seeds and insects. Once common across Europe and Asia, their numbers have drastically declined due to illegal trapping for food and habitat loss. They are now critically endangered.

Fun Fact: Yellow-breasted Buntings are sometimes called "Rice Birds" because they love feasting on rice grains during migration.

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Yellow-breasted Bunting
  • Scientific Name: Emberiza aureola
  • Animal Type: Bird
  • Size: 14-15 cm (5.5-5.9 in.)
  • Weight: 20-30 grams (0.04-0.07 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 4-6 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered

2. Christmas Island Frigatebirds

Fregata andrewsi

Christmas Island Frigatebird is a graceful seabird with long, slender wings and a forked tail that make it an excellent flier. Males have a bright red throat pouch that they inflate like a balloon to attract females during the breeding season. Females are larger and have white feathers on their bellies. These birds spend most of their lives soaring over the ocean, hunting for fish and squid, which they snatch from the water's surface. They nest only on Christmas Island, making them very vulnerable to habitat loss and invasive species.

Fun Fact: Male Christmas Island Frigatebirds put on a spectacular show by inflating their red throat pouches, making them look like floating red balloons!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Christmas Island Frigatebird
  • Scientific Name: Fregata andrewsi
  • Animal Type: Bird
  • Size: 85-100 cm (33-39 in.)
  • Wingspan: 2.1-2.3 meters (6.9-7.5 ft)
  • Weight: 1.5-1.8 kg (3.3-4 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 25-30 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered

3. Siberian Crane

Grus leucogeranus

Siberian Crane is a tall, elegant bird with snow-white feathers and a vivid red face, making it one of the most beautiful cranes in the world. These birds are expert flyers, migrating thousands of kilometers between their breeding grounds in Siberia and their wintering areas in China and Iran. Standing about 140 cm (4.6 feet) tall, they have a wingspan of up to 2.3 meters (7.5 feet), making them graceful gliders in the sky.

Siberian Cranes depend on wetlands for survival, where they feed on aquatic plants, insects, and small fish. Sadly, this critically endangered bird is threatened by habitat loss and hunting along its migration routes. Efforts are underway to protect their habitats and ensure their long-term survival.

Fun Fact: Siberian Cranes are known for their loud, trumpet-like calls, which can be heard from miles away. It's like nature's music in the wild!

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Siberian Crane
  • Scientific Name: Grus leucogeranus
  • Animal Type: Bird
  • Size: Up to 140 cm (4.6 ft)
  • Wingspan: Up to 2.3 meters (7.5 ft)
  • Weight: 4.9-8.6 kg (10.8-19 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 15-20 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered
