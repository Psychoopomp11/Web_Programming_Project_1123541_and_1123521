8. White-rumped Vulture

Gyps bengalensis

White-rumped Vulture is a medium-sized scavenger bird that plays a critical role in keeping ecosystems clean by feeding on carcasses. It has a dark body with white patches on its rump and under its wings, making it easy to identify during flight. These vultures are social birds, often seen in groups near food sources.

White-rumped Vultures are found in the Indian subcontinent, where they nest in tall trees or cliffs. Unfortunately, they are critically endangered due to poisoning from a veterinary drug called diclofenac, which is toxic to vultures when they consume treated livestock carcasses.

Fun Fact: White-rumped Vultures have incredible eyesight, allowing them to spot food from great distances in the sky.

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): White-rumped Vulture
  • Scientific Name: Gyps bengalensis
  • Animal Type: Bird
  • Size: 75-85 cm (29-33 in.)
  • Wingspan: Up to 2.6 meters (8.5 ft)
  • Weight: 3.5-7.5 kg (7.7-16.5 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered

9.Indian Vulture

Gyps indicus

Indian Vulture is a large bird known for its pale, cream-colored body and bald head, perfectly suited for its scavenger lifestyle. These vultures are found mainly in the Indian subcontinent, where they soar over forests and open areas in search of carrion.

Like their White-rumped cousins, Indian Vultures face critical threats from diclofenac poisoning, which has drastically reduced their population. Conservation programs are focused on creating safe zones and banning harmful drugs to protect these important birds.

  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered

10. Slender-billed Vulture

Gyps tenuirostris

Slender-billed Vulture is a critically endangered bird with a long, slender neck and a thin bill, ideal for picking apart carcasses. It is closely related to the Indian Vulture and shares much of the same habitat and behavior. These vultures are found in South and Southeast Asia, particularly in forests and open plains.

Slender-billed Vultures are vital for maintaining healthy ecosystems by disposing of animal remains. However, like other vulture species, they are severely threatened by diclofenac poisoning, habitat loss, and reduced food availability.

Fun Fact: Slender-billed Vultures are incredibly light for their size, allowing them to glide effortlessly in the sky for hours.

Quick Fact Sheet

  • Common Name(s): Slender-billed Vulture
  • Scientific Name: Gyps tenuirostris
  • Animal Type: Bird
  • Size: 80-100 cm (31-39 in.)
  • Wingspan: Up to 2.6 meters (8.5 ft)
  • Weight: 5.5-6.3 kg (12-14 lbs)
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years in the wild
  • Conservation Status: Critically Endangered
